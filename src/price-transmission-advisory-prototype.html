Price-transmission decision-support — working prototype

Should I act on this price move?

Pick a market pair below to see the two judgments a price-transmission-aware advisory would need to get right: whether a hub-market move is worth acting on, and whether a current price gap can be expected to close.

Scope: this reads out results already computed and validated on USDA NASS corn prices, Jan 2015–May 2026, for exactly two pairs — Iowa–Ohio and Iowa–Nebraska. It is not connected to live prices and does not cover other states or commodities yet.

Settlement advisory — Iowa → Ohio

Corn, monthly
2015–2026 (137 mo.)
Is a move in Iowa's price worth acting on?
Act on it

Yes. When Iowa's price moves, Ohio's moves the same month, and Iowa's recent price history helps predict where Ohio's is headed for roughly the next eight months.

same-month lag: k*=0, ρ=0.840, 95% CI [0,0]
Iowa→Ohio Granger: significant lags 1–8 (min p=0.0089)
Ohio→Iowa: not supported
Will the Iowa–Ohio price gap close?
Use caution

Not reliably. Over the past ten years this gap has sometimes closed and sometimes just drifted, with no stable pattern. Don't assume the current gap will revert.

cointegration (full period): p=0.1545, not significant
rolling 5-yr windows: significant in only 20 of 78 (26%)
structural break test: none detected (p=0.82)

Settlement advisory — Iowa → Nebraska

Corn, monthly
2015–2026 (137 mo.)
Is a move in Iowa's price worth acting on?
Act on it

Yes, even more so than Ohio. Iowa and Nebraska move together the same month, and Iowa's history predicts Nebraska's returns across the full 12-month window tested.

same-month lag: k*=0, ρ=0.918, 95% CI [0,0]
Iowa→Nebraska Granger: significant lags 1–12 (min p<0.001)
Nebraska→Iowa: not supported (p up to 0.995)
Will the Iowa–Nebraska price gap close?
Expect reversion

Yes, with real confidence. This gap has a long, stable history of closing back up, confirmed again on a held-out period held back from model-building. A shift in the relationship itself was detected in September 2022 — worth knowing, but it does not mean the gap stopped reverting.

cointegration (full period): p=0.0082, significant
stable across backtest split (train p=0.0415)
structural break detected: Sept. 2022 (p<0.001)
This is a working prototype, not a live tool or a finished product. It reads out results already computed and validated in “Toward Price-Transmission-Aware Decision Support for Corn Belt Farmers: An Empirical Diagnosis.” Extending it to Illinois, Minnesota, Wisconsin, and soybeans — and connecting it to a live price feed — is the planned next step, not something this prototype does today. Data: USDA NASS QuickStats, Price Received (corn), Jan 2015–May 2026.
